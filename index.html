How it works

Problem first. Thoughtful vetting. Light builds. Real school signals. Scale the ones that work.

01

Problem First

Our automated Product Idea Ingestion Engine (PIIE) enables users to systematically organize their ideas. The process is confidential; your idea will not be submitted until you choose to do so. Upon completion, you will receive a comprehensive concept memo that documents the specific school challenge you face each week and outlines a proposed solution. You may save, print, or share this memo with yourself, and submit it to us for review at your convenience. The PIIE assists in mapping out workflow processes, identifying breakdowns, and highlighting relevant constraints such as time, staffing, compliance, and privacy. Additionally, common failure modes are clearly defined to ensure solutions are grounded in practical realities rather than idealized scenarios.

02

Idea Coaching

We pressure-test the idea with industry experts, investigate what already exists, why it falls short, and what a realistic "version 1" could be. We also decide what should remain human-led and where technology truly helps. The aim is to create a practical plan that results in a solution schools are likely to implement.

03

Light Build

We build a small, focused MVP designed around real school routines and usability. You give quick feedback as we build so we don't drift into "features that look good in demos." The result is a usable first version—not a bloated platform.

04

Real-World Signals

We test the tool in real meetings and routines to see if it reduces burden and improves follow-through. We look for adoption signals (do people keep using it) and practical value (does it save time or improve execution). We also check willingness to pay so the solution can sustain.

05

Scale or Stop

If it earns real momentum, we improve it, harden it, and expand it to more schools and districts. If it doesn't, we stop quickly and learn why and consider how to overcome those challenges. Tools that prove practical value get more funding and engineering.

How we evaluate ideas

This isn't a competition. Good ideas aren't "rejected" — they're clarified and refined. We look for ideas that can genuinely reduce burden and improve execution in real schools.

Problem Clarity + Frequency

Is this pain recurring and widely felt?

Impact

Does it improve follow-through, efficiency, equity, or student support?

Feasibility

Can it work inside school constraints (time, staffing, policy, privacy)?

Adoption Likelihood

Would leaders actually use it weekly in real routines?

Focus

Can it ship as a practical tool (not a massive platform)?

What to expect

  • ✓ Clear communication and defined next steps
  • ✓ Practical timelines (built around school calendars)
  • ✓ A realistic definition of "version 1"
  • ✓ If an idea doesn't move forward, you still walk away with sharper clarity and a professional concept summary
